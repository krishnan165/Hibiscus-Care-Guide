Hibiscus Care Guide

Soil for Hibiscus

Well-draining, fertile soil with a slightly acidic to neutral pH.

Hibiscus plants thrive in soil that provides good drainage, fertility, and has a slightly acidic to neutral pH.

Watering

Regular watering, keeping the soil consistently moist.

Keep the soil evenly moist, especially during dry periods, to support the growth of hibiscus plants.

Pruning

Prune to shape and remove dead or overgrown branches.

Regular pruning helps maintain a tidy appearance and promotes healthy growth.

Colors

Benefits

Large, vibrant flowers in various colors.

Hibiscus is known for its stunning and colorful flowers that attract attention in the garden.

Attracts hummingbirds and butterflies.

The vibrant blooms of hibiscus are attractive to pollinators, enhancing the biodiversity of your garden.

Non-Benefits

May be sensitive to cold temperatures; protect the plant during frost.
